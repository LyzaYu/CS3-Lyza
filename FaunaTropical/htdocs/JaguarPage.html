JAGUAR

Jaguars are the only big cat in the Americas and the third biggest in the world after tigers and lions. They look a lot like leopards, which live in Africa and Asia, but jaguars’ spots are more complex and often have a dot in the center.
These powerful cats were worshipped as gods in many ancient South American cultures, and representations of the jaguar show up in the art and archaeology of pre-Columbian cultures across the jaguar’s range.
Unlike many other cats, jaguars do not avoid water.
Jaguars also eat deer, peccaries, capybaras, tapirs, and a number of other land animals, which they prefer to ambush at night.

Jaguar - The True King of The Jungle
  • Scientific Name: Panthera onca

  • Diet: Carnivore

  • Average Lifespan: 12 to 15 years

  • Speed: 80km/h max

  • Mass: 56 to 96kg (adult)

  • Conservation Status: Near Theathened (Decreasing population)

  • Class: Mammalia

  • Family: Felidae

  • Genus: Panthera

  • Kingdom: Animalia

Fun Facts:

  • They are quite good swimmers.

  • Their jaws can pierce animals' skulls.

  • They live alone.

  • They mark their territory by their waste or clawing trees.

  • Females have litters of one to four cubs.

  • The mother may defend even from the cubs' own father.

  • Since the 1880s, they’ve lost more than half their territory.

  • They are found in tropical rainforests, grasslands, and savannas.
